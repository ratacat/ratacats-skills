# Human Tweet Rubric

Use this rubric for scoring, critique, and rewrite decisions.

## 100-Point Score

### Human Voice - 25

Strong:
- Sounds like one identifiable person with taste, context, and stakes.
- Allows small asymmetries: a casual aside, a concrete annoyance, a restrained joke, a real hesitation.
- Uses normal language the author would plausibly say out loud.

Weak:
- Sounds like a LinkedIn carousel compressed into a tweet.
- Uses balanced slogan pairs, inflated abstractions, or three-part marketing rhythm.
- Feels allergic to specificity.

### Specificity And Evidence - 20

Strong:
- Names the product, workflow, bug, customer, benchmark, failure mode, or observation.
- Gives enough context to make the claim inspectable.
- Uses numbers only when real.

Weak:
- "AI is changing everything."
- "Builders are moving faster than ever."
- "This will transform how teams work."

### Hook And Opening Pressure - 15

Strong:
- First line creates a concrete reason to continue.
- Starts close to the interesting part.
- Avoids setup that only exists to sound important.

Weak:
- Starts with "Excited to announce", "In today's fast-paced world", "Here are 7 lessons".
- Opens with a conclusion before giving any reason to trust it.

### Substance - 15

Strong:
- Contains an actual point: a tradeoff, lesson, result, demo, failure, or opinion.
- Gives the reader something to steal, argue with, or remember.

Weak:
- Makes a vibe claim.
- Repeats the hook with different words.

### Audience Fit - 10

Strong:
- Speaks to builders, technical founders, devtool users, AI practitioners, or software people without explaining obvious basics.
- Uses the right density for the audience.

Weak:
- Explains too much for experts or too little for outsiders.
- Uses generic founder Twitter language without technical grip.

### Engagement Affordance - 10

Strong:
- Invites a natural reply because the claim is specific, useful, debatable, or personally resonant.
- The best reply path is obvious without begging.

Weak:
- "Thoughts?"
- "Agree?"
- "Like and repost."

### Mechanics - 5

Strong:
- Fits X constraints.
- Easy to scan.
- No accidental ambiguity, broken grammar, or dead links in the main claim.

Weak:
- Too many clauses.
- Too many hashtags.
- Main claim hidden after a long preamble.

## AI-Sounding Tells

Penalize heavily for:

- "unlock", "elevate", "delve", "seamless", "robust", "game-changer", "cutting-edge", "transformative"
- "not just X, but Y"
- "whether you're a..."
- "in the world of..."
- symmetrical slogan cadence
- fake certainty
- inflated stakes unsupported by evidence
- generic advice without a real scene

## Rewrite Moves

- Replace claims with scenes: "AI makes devs faster" -> "I watched a junior ship a working admin panel before lunch."
- Replace adjectives with evidence: "powerful tool" -> "it found the migration bug I missed twice."
- Replace generic audience with a precise one: "teams" -> "two-person SaaS teams with no infra person."
- Replace broad lesson with a narrow lesson: "ship faster" -> "ship the boring first version before designing permissions."
- Replace CTA with a real question: "Thoughts?" -> "Where does this break once the repo has real users?"
